Your distress summons Bụt once more. He appears in a swirl of clouds and says:
“Child, bring the baskets outside.
I will call the sparrows to help you.”
You carry the baskets into the courtyard.
In moments, a flock of sparrows swoops down and separates everything perfectly.
The task is finished, but you burst into tears again — you’re still wearing old rags.
How can you go to the festival like this?
