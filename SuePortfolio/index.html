Artist Statement

My name is Sue Wari, and I am a Visual Arts student at Fordham University. I aim to explore the significance of the spaces we inhabit and build. My work draws inspiration from human experience, examining how our emotions and inner states are reflected in the environments we create.

I am particularly interested in the intersection between the physical space and the psychological space we occupy, and how these elements influence our perception of the world around us.

As a student also pursuing a minor in Writing, I enjoy bridging the worlds of the visual and the textual, incorporating narrative elements into my artwork to deepen the viewer's connection with the piece. My work primarily uses mixed media, combining sketching, charcoal, and digital tools like Photoshop, to create layered and dynamic expressions of these themes. Through this combination of mediums, I aim to evoke both the tangible and intangible aspects of human experience, inviting the viewer to reflect on the emotional resonance of the spaces they occupy.
